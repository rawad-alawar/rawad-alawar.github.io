Connecting

One method for building trust that I plan to use immediately is the Three Assumptions. When I was reading that portion of the chapter, I thought about how the meetings I have in my week now and if I am applying the three assumptions at every meeting. In particular, I plan on applying this method to the next check in. The Three Assumption are:

  1. Assume that everybody in this room is here to serve the greater good, until proven otherwise.
  2. Given the above assumption, we therefore assume that none of us has any hidden agenda, until proven otherwise.
  3. Given the above assumption, we therefore assume that we are all reasonable even when we disagree, until proven otherwise.
Using these three assumptions will help me trust and understand my cohort mates. The first one seems easy enough because as I've gotten to know my classmates, I have become increasingly convinced that we are all here to serve the greater good, not just ourselves. Furthermore, I can not expect others to believe my intentions if I do not believe in theirs. The second and third assumptions are also important because they ensure we are all judged fairly and with understanding; they help us overcome any tendency to label an idea as "stupid" without first understanding the other persons perspective. I believe once I apply these assumptions to other people, I will never come across a "stupid" idea again.

A Time I Got Blocked & Elegantly Solved the Problem

The problem:

I got blocked on a problem that required us to make a chess board of any n by n size. I had no idea where to start.

Technique used to solve the problem:

I used a less detailed form of psuedocode to logically break down the problem. I pulled out a pencil and some paper and sketched out the board. I then assigned the rows and columns to a variable. Then, I wrote down the relationship between different coordinates on the board. Once I had a pattern down, I got to writing the code. After a couple of hours, I managed to solve the problem.

How I felt throughout the process:

I alternated between frustration and elation. Every breakthrough was so satisfying.

What did I learn:

I learned not to give up and not to go straight to asking for help.

Types of Problem Solving Techniques

  • Psuedocode-I am comfortable using psuedocode to solve problems because it is one of the methods I learned in college.
  • Trying Something- I have used this method for nearly every bit of code I have written because I am not 100% confident that what I type is what I want the code to do.
  • Rubber Ducky Method- I don't use this method out of laziness.
  • Reading Error Messages - I am confident in using this method and do so often. Whenever I receive an error message that I don't understand, I immediately Google it.
  • Console.log- I use this method most often when running loops to see if the code is outputting in the order I want.
  • Googling- I do this all the time.
  • Asking for Help- I am still not fully confident when it comes to using Slack or other communication forms to ask others for help.
  • Improving with Reflection- I reflect quite a bit and go over the old material often.
